Research and Work Experience

Graduate Research Assistant, Case Western Reserve University, Cleveland, Ohio, USA (Sep 2021-Present)
  • NASA Funded Project: Understanding Burning Behaviors of Solid Materials in Micro and Partial Gravities
    • Apparatus Development:
      • Refurbished a vacuum combustion chamber capable of sustaining a wide range of pressure (0-300 kPa), ensuring precise control over ambient conditions for concurrent and opposed flame spread experiments.
      • Designed a sample mounting system for thermally thin fuel samples for consistent positioning and uniform ignition.
      • Established different ambient environmental conditions using vacuum pumps, compressed gas inlets, and PID-controlled valves.
    • Experimental Work:
      • Conducted flame spread experiments over thin cellulosic materials in a wide range of pressure (10-250 kPa) and oxygen (9-21%) conditions.
      • Developed MATLAB code to track and analyze flame progression, extracting flame spread rate, heating length, and flame height.
      • Compiled datasets from literature to contextualize flame spread behavior across various environments and fuels.
    • Key Findings:
      • Flame spread rate and burning intensity decrease with lower ambient pressure and oxygen.
      • Some flame behaviors in microgravity and partial gravity can be observed in Earth's gravity under reduced pressure.
      • Opposed flow flame spreads are up to 30 times slower than concurrent flow under similar conditions.
  • Wildland Urban Interface Fire Hazards Project
    • Apparatus Development:
      • Modified a TPP test apparatus to evaluate fire blankets, using a calibrated Meker burner and specimen holder for repeatable tests.
      • Integrated thermocouples and a water-cooled calorimeter for detailed temperature and heat flux measurements.
    • Experimental Work:
      • Tested fire blankets (SW-HD fiberglass and ALFPN 1501 aramid) in single/double-layer configurations under identical heat exposure.
      • Analyzed charring progression and quantified charring rates.
    • Key Findings:
      • Higher TPP rating blankets provided superior protection and slower charring rates.
      • Double layers enhanced protection, reducing charring rates by up to 44.7% for aramid and 24.1% for fiberglass blankets.
  • NASA Funded Project: Thermal analysis and pyrolysis modelling of NOMEX fabric
    • Conducted thermogravimetric analysis of Nomex fabric under various oxygen environments and heating rates.
    • Applied Flynn-Wall-Ozawa and Kissinger's models to extract activation energy.
    • Key findings: Atmospheric composition strongly influences NOMEX pyrolysis; higher heating rates shift decomposition temperatures and increase mass loss rate; activation energies vary by environment.
  • NASA Collaboration Project: Numerical study of droplet heating to study “cool” flames
    • Developed ANSYS Fluent models to study influence of insertion speed on n-dodecane droplet autoignition.
    • Simulated particle temperature across different insertion speeds.
  • Additional Responsibilities:
    • Performed standardized fire testing (ASTM/ISO) with Cone Calorimeter, Smoke Density Chamber, Micro-Cone Calorimeter, and TPP Apparatus.
