CFD Project 3: Comparison of Lid-Driven Cavity Flow for Re=100 and Re=1000

Introduction

As discussed in previous project, the lid-driven cavity is a canonical benchmark problem in computational fluid dynamics for validating Navier-Stokes equation solvers. This problem consists of a square cavity containing fluid, with the top wall moving at unit velocity while the other three walls remain stationary. The moving lid generates a complex recirculating flow pattern with primary and secondary vortices, making it an ideal test case for numerical accuracy and efficiency across a range of Reynolds numbers.

The simplicity of the geometry combined with well-defined boundary conditions makes this problem widely used in the CFD community. By simulating this flow at different Reynolds numbers (Re = 100 and 1000), we can observe how inertial effects progressively dominate over viscous effects, leading to increasingly complex flow patterns.

Problem Formulation

Governing Equations

Continuity Equation (Mass Conservation):

\[ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0 \]

Momentum Equation (Navier-Stokes):

\[ \frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla)\mathbf{u} = -\nabla p + \frac{1}{Re}\nabla^2 \mathbf{u} \]

Domain and Boundary Conditions

Unit Square Domain: [0,1] × [0,1]

Top wall (y = 1): \(u = 1, v = 0\) (moving lid)
Bottom wall (y = 0): \(u = 0, v = 0\) (no-slip)
Left wall (x = 0): \(u = 0, v = 0\) (no-slip)
Right wall (x = 1): \(u = 0, v = 0\) (no-slip)

Solution Methodology

Numerical Approach: Fractional Step Method

The Navier-Stokes equations are solved using the fractional step (projection) method, which decouples the velocity and pressure calculations. This method consists of three main steps:

Step 1: Advection-Diffusion Equation

\[ \frac{\mathbf{u}^* - \mathbf{u}^n}{\Delta t} = -(\mathbf{u}^n \cdot \nabla)\mathbf{u}^n + \frac{1}{Re}\nabla^2\mathbf{u}^* \]

Step 2: Pressure Poisson Equation

\[ \nabla^2 p^{n+1} = \frac{\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u}^*}{\Delta t} \]

Step 3: Velocity Correction

\[ \mathbf{u}^{n+1} = \mathbf{u}^* - \Delta t \nabla p^{n+1} \]

Discretization Scheme

A mixed implicit-explicit scheme in time with second-order central differences in space is employed for spatial and temporal discretization. The spatial grid uses a cell-centered collocated arrangement, which places all velocity components and pressure at the same grid points, ensuring stable and accurate numerical solutions.

The advection-diffusion equation is discretized using a blend of explicit treatment for convective (nonlinear) terms and implicit treatment for diffusive terms. This approach maintains numerical stability while capturing the nonlinear advection effects. The discretized equations for both \(x\)- and \(y\)-direction momentum components incorporate explicit approximations of the convective terms using \(3/2\Delta t\) and \(1/2\Delta t\) weighted evaluations at different time levels to achieve second-order accuracy in time.

The pressure Poisson equation is solved at each time step using either a point-wise Gauss-Seidel iteration or a line-based Gauss-Seidel with Successive Over-Relaxation (Line-SOR) method. The central difference approximation for spatial derivatives ensures \(O(\Delta x^2, \Delta y^2)\) accuracy, resulting in overall accuracy of \(O(\Delta x^2, \Delta y^2, \Delta t)\) for the advection-diffusion step and \(O(\Delta x^2, \Delta y^2)\) for the pressure and correction steps. The scheme is consistent since as \(\Delta x, \Delta y, \Delta t \to 0\), the truncation error approaches zero.
